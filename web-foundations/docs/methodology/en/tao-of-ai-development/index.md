### The Seven Signs of Garbage Code

1. **The Unnamed Horror**: Variables called `x`, `temp`, `data`, `thing`

3. **The Comment Lie**: `// This function calculates tax` (it does not)
4. **The Dependency Hydra**: `node_modules` larger than the project itself
5. **The Copy-Paste Plague**: The same code, slightly different, in 47 places
6. **The Magic Number Cult**: `if (status === 42)` with no explanation
7. **The Dead Code Cemetery**: Commented blocks that "might be needed later"

## Chapter VI: Koans for the AI Age

### Koan 1: The Empty Context Window

A student asked the master: "My AI gives wrong answers. What should I do?"

The master replied: "Did you give it the right questions?"

The student said: "I asked it to fix my code."

The master said: "Did you tell it what your code should do?"

The student was silent.

The master said: "The AI is a mirror. It reflects what you give it. Give it garbage, receive garbage. Give it clarity, receive clarity. The context window is not the AI's limitationâ€”it is yours."

### Koan 2: The Passing Test

A student celebrated: "Master! All my tests pass!"

The master asked: "What do your tests test?"

The student said: "That the code works."

The master asked: "What does 'works' mean?"

The student said: "It returns the expected output."

The master asked: "Expected by whom?"

The student fell silent.

The master said: "A test that passes is not proof of correctness. It is proof that you have not yet imagined the ways it can fail."

### Koan 3: The Confident Hallucination

A student complained: "The AI told me to use `leftPad`. But `leftPad` broke the internet in 2016!"

The master smiled: "And did you check?"

The student said: "No, the AI seemed confident."

The master said: "Confidence is not truth. The AI does not know what it does not know. It speaks with the same tone whether it is correct or hallucinating. Your job is to know the difference. The AI's confidence is infinite. Your skepticism must match it."

### Koan 4: The Attribution

A student submitted code. The grader asked: "Did you write this?"

The student said: "The AI wrote it."

The grader asked: "Then who learned?"

The student said: "I learned to prompt."

The grader said: "Prompting is not programming. Understanding is programming. Can you explain this code?"

The student could not.

The grader said: "Then you have submitted someone else's work that you do not understand. This is not learning. This is laundering."
